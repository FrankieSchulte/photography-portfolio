# Content Checklist

The starter deliberately withholds facts that were not supplied. Collect and approve the following before launch.

## Identity and contact

- Professional name or studio name.
- City/region and accurate travel language.
- Public email address.
- Instagram handle and approved additional links.
- Real domain name.
- Copyright name and preferred image-use notice.

## Biography and approach

- A short homepage/about excerpt.
- A first-person biography of roughly 120–180 words.
- A practical paragraph about preparation, collaboration, on-location behavior, editing, and delivery.
- Years of experience only if you want them public and can state them accurately.
- A real headshot or behind-the-scenes portrait.

## Concert work

- Strongest lead image.
- Two or three complete show sequences, ideally five to seven frames each.
- Standalone hero images that add a new scale, light, emotion, or storytelling function.
- Approved artist, venue/festival, city, year, client/publication, and usage information.
- Evidence for every service claim you keep: live coverage, tour documentation, festivals, press/editorial, backstage, or artist portraits.

## Supporting work

- Portrait selections split conceptually into Observed and Constructed.
- Event sequences that show context, candid moments, details, groups, speakers/stage where relevant, and a closing atmosphere frame.
- Graduation selections with privacy approval.
- Field Notes selections with place/year captions when known.

## Credibility

Add only real and approved items:

- Clients, artists, venues, festivals, publications, brands, or institutions.
- Awards, exhibitions, books, covers, campaigns, or press.
- Two or three concise testimonials at most.

Omit the section when nothing appropriate is available. Empty space is more credible than invented proof.

## Inquiry policy

- Project types you actually accept.
- Accurate service area and travel availability.
- Whether a budget question is helpful.
- Whether starting prices should be public.
- Response-time language you can consistently meet.
- A real form endpoint only if desired; direct email remains the fallback.
